Background

The choice of charity for a website design is Pets for Life Animal Shelter, a non-profit organisation that deals in the rehabilitation of stray cats in Billinudgel in NSW. Annually re-homing over 100 cats and kittens in Byron Shire, they are a non-euthanising shelter, micro-chipping, vaccinating and de-sexing strays upon admission and ready for foster or adoption care. The shelter operates on volunteer work, and relies on the donations from patrons for maintenance and care.

Research

Like most not-for-profit organisations (NFP), it faces funding and awareness issues. This results in higher fees for adoption of stray cats.The advent of COVID19 also has not helped, putting a halt to many NFP schemes. (Kelloway, 2020) Conventional methods of fundraising like physical meetups, bake sales, second hand good events are cancelled, in favor of virtual alternatives that limit physical contact.

Thus, creating a website for Pets for Life Animal Shelter offers an interesting opportunity to unite the cat owning community together. With a community portal website, users can meet and make friends with fellow owners through the interaction of their pets profiles, whilst the NFP can manage donations and community engagement through one social channel. An online portal allows opportunities for virtual fundraisers encouraging users to donate to the shelter, even during situations like a pandemic.

Ideation

Ideation started with planning out the architechture of the website. Navigation of the community portal started with planning out the sitemap.

HOME

Home functions similarly to a landing page, where users can access information relating to the organisation, donate and login/sign up to the website community portal.

ABOUT US

The About Us tab contains contact information, history of the shelter and a google map for users to be guided to the shelter for physical visits. Users can also find social media links and a link to the main website.

DONATE

The Donate tab allows users to access different methods of donating:

  • ONE TIME
  • RECURRING
Each selection will bring users to a donation form where they can fill out with their details and donate to the shelter.

LOGIN/SIGNUP

The Login/signup screen leads users to login or sign up to the community portal. The login/signup forms on the screen are interchangable for both site visitors and users to interact with.

USER PROFILE HOME

  • CAT PROFILE

    Profile at the side of the page will display info of the user's cat including information like
    • Name
    • Birthday
    • Type of Cat
    • Pictures and Videos of Cat
    • Owner Name


  • NEWS FEED

    In the middle of the page will be a scrollable feed where users can view and interact with posts by other users friended or the shelter.

  • FIND MY FURRY FRIENDS

    A search bar exists on the right side of the screen, where users can look up owners and their cats to connect with and follow. Users can also follow users who are biologically related to their cat.

  • FURRY FRIENDS

    Under the search bar box exists a list that users have connected with to message.
FIND MY FRIENDS

Once the user has searched up a cat or user, they will be navigated to a page of list of searches where they can connect with various users.

Sketches

The initial design for the community portal had an interface similar to social media networks like Facebook. According to a survey of statista showed that 68% of U.S. Baby Boomer adults ( ages 50 - 64 ) used Facebook as of June 2019. As this demographic covers most of our user profiles, it would be ideal to design a portal with a similar layout for users to adapt to a familiar interface.

The following images illustrate a proposed look for the website portal. A simple, minimalistic look with fewer buttons would allow for an easier, intutive navigation experience our proposed users. (Berman, Grossman, Scardino, Mickelson & Shamosh, 2017)
